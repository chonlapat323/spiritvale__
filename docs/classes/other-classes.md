# Other Classes

Brief profile + playstyle direction for the remaining advanced classes. Less deep-dived than [paladin.md](paladin.md) and [weaver.md](weaver.md) — no per-skill data (cooldown/mana/dmg) gathered yet for these.

## Berserker
From Warrior, Lv 26-30, Windy Desert. "A frenzied warrior who feeds on the fight itself, growing more dangerous the harder and bloodier it gets."
Skills: Mighty Roar, Earth Splitter, Dark Claw, Wild Charge, Blood Howl, Furious Shout, Execute, Brutality, Cyclone, Fearsome Cry, Berserk, Axe Throw, Blood Frenzy, Increased Regeneration, Blood Crash, Unyielding

**Playstyle direction:** Aggressive melee glass cannon — damage scales with taking/dealing damage (Blood Frenzy), so play recklessly on purpose rather than defensively. Best for players who like high-risk, high-reward brawling and don't mind dying if the read is wrong. Weak early in a fight before the "frenzy" stacks build up.

## Priest
From Acolyte, Wayfarer's Landing. "A devoted cleric who turns faith into a fortress — potent heals, blessings, and wards that keep a party standing."
Skills: High Heal, Salvation, Status Recovery, Turn Undead, Smite, Holy Wrath, Sanctuary, Sacred Ground, Guardian Spirit, Mace Mastery

**Playstyle direction:** Pure support/healer — bring this if you want to play in a party and keep others alive rather than deal damage yourself. Weak for solo leveling/farming since there's no real damage identity in the kit. Not recommended as a first/only character if you plan to solo much of the game.

## Wizard
From Mage, Lv 36-40, Mystic Lake 2. "Unleash devastating elemental magic, reshaping the field with sheer power."
14 skills across elements — Fire: Fire Pillar, Avatar of Fire, Meteor | Lightning: Chain Lightning, Tempest, Avatar of Storm | Ice: Blizzard, Avatar of Frost | Earth: Earthquake, Earth Wall, Avatar of Stone | Multi: Elemental Overload, Hydro Vortex, Arcane Sigil

**Playstyle direction:** Classic AOE elemental burst mage — swap element per target's weakness, nuke groups from range. Good default pick for straightforward, effective PvE farming. Squishy — avoid melee range, kite.

## Shinobi
From Rogue, Lv 86-90, Underground Cavern. "Slip past defenses to land lethal, precise strikes."
16 skills — Flame Orb, Binding Spiral, Flash Step, elemental releases (Fire/Ice/Lightning), Shadow Seal, Silent Edge, Black Blade, Shuriken Fan, Elusive Feint

**Playstyle direction:** Mobile single-target burst/assassin — strong for PvP and solo bosses via high mobility (Flash Step, Elusive Feint) and precision hits. Weaker against groups/AOE-heavy content since the kit leans single-target.

## Gunslinger
From Scout, Lv 31-35, Windy Desert North. "A deadeye gunner who rains rapid, ranged fire, controlling engagements with raw firepower and precision." Received a **complete rework** in the latest patch — see [../patch-notes.md](../patch-notes.md).
19 skills — Piercing Shot, Fan Fire, grenades (Explosive/Poison/Freeze), shots (Jump/Sniper/Aerial), Trigger Happy, Lockdown, Suppressive Shot (ultimate), Gun Mastery, Dual Loadout

**Playstyle direction:** Ranged burst + control hybrid, now with a Heavy Loadout (Launcher/Gatling) as a 3rd weapon set. Rewards players who like managing loadout swaps and building burst windows (stand still to stack Lockdown, then unload). Good pick if you want ranged combat with more decision-making than a straightforward nuker.

## Necromancer
From Summoner, Lv 26-30, Festering Woods 2. "A dark summoner who commands the dead and drains the living, winning through attrition and decay."
Skills: Abomination, Skeleton, Skeleton Mage, Wraith, Reanimation, Bone Spear, Bone Spikes, Death Coil, Corpse Explosion, Corpse Barrier, Life Drain, Soul Drain, Harvest, Reap, Death Nova, Death Spiral, Necrotic Presence

**Playstyle direction:** Minion-based attrition + lifesteal — passive, sustained playstyle rather than active burst. Risk: minion AI/pathing quality is an unknown and could make or break this class in practice. Recently shifted to the new Ghost element (see [../patch-notes.md](../patch-notes.md)) — outcome (buff or nerf) depends on how common Ghost resistance turns out to be on monsters.
